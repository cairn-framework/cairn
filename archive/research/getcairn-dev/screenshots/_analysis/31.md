## Summary
This screenshot shows a CAIRN map view scrolled into the body of an ROV (remotely operated vehicle) system decomposition. The visible panels include a tail of a "Parts & Assemblies" grouping, an "Instruments & Connections" interface section, and a "Knowledge Foundation" lane populated with constraint cards. Cards are tagged by category (PERFORMANCE, ENVIRONMENTAL, FUNCTIONAL, SAFETY) and colored accordingly. The composition demonstrates how CAIRN renders both physical decomposition (parts) and abstract constraints (knowledge) on the same canvas.

## Novel observations
- "GAP / needs decomposition" appears as a first-class card state, signalling unfilled subtree work directly inside the part card.
- A "Knowledge Foundation" lane sits beside parts and interfaces, modelling requirements/constraints as graph nodes (not external docs).
- Each constraint card carries a top-tag taxonomy: PERFORMANCE, ENVIRONMENTAL, FUNCTIONAL, SAFETY, plus a category footer label. This dual labelling (top tag + bottom slug) is a portable pattern.
- Domain-specific vocabulary: "LARS" (launch and recovery system), "COLREGS" (maritime collision regs), "tether management", "edge processing compression", "satellite uplink" appear as concrete constraint titles.
- Section captions on the right ("interfaces, protocols, and data channels", "Requirements, constraints, and understanding") provide layperson-friendly glosses next to the formal lane name.
- Color encoding: pink/red for GAP/decomposition-needed parts, cream for resolved interface and knowledge cards. Suggests a status-by-color convention.
