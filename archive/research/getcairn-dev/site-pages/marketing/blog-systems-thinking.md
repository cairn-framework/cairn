# Every Hard Problem Left Is a Systems Problem

**Source:** https://www.getcairn.dev/blog/systems-thinking
**Captured:** 2026-04-28

## Thesis

AI is compressing the digital layer into a commodity. The engineers who hold complex physical systems in their heads are about to become the most important people in the room.

Here's what nobody in tech is discussing: "[the software revolution is eating itself](/)". LLMs and AI agents are automating code generation, collapsing the marginal cost of software production toward zero.

When code stops being the limiting factor, the world shifts from digital to physical infrastructure. The hardest remaining challenges (autonomous vehicles, clean energy grids, space infrastructure, defense platforms, medical devices) aren't software problems. They're systems problems requiring individuals who can simultaneously understand hardware, software, physics, human factors, and constraints.

"[The ability to decompose a complex system into layers of causality](/), to see not just what exists, but what's missing, is the one skill that gets more valuable as AI gets more powerful."

## The Tooling Crisis Nobody Talks About

Systems engineers rely on tools designed before smartphones existed, expensive software requiring months to master. "[Enterprise platforms built for the procurement era, not the engineering era](/)" still treat model-based systems engineering as secondary to documentation.

The $7 billion MBSE market grows 15%+ annually, yet "[the tooling serving it hasn't had a meaningful innovation in over a decade](/)". The incumbents charge $600 to $2,500 per user yearly for outdated software.

## Why This Matters Right Now

**01 - AI as Thinking Partner**

Technology now exists for AI to reason about system decomposition, dependencies, constraints, and tradeoffs, not just document summaries. Yet the entire AI tooling ecosystem targets software developers, leaving systems engineers underserved.

**02 - The Atoms Economy**

Defense spending, commercialized space, rebuilt energy infrastructure, and record hardware investment create simultaneous demand across sectors. Supply of rigorous systems thinkers cannot keep pace.

**03 - The Knowledge Cliff**

"[Experienced systems engineers are retiring faster than they're being replaced](/)", institutional knowledge about failure modes and interface breakdowns is disappearing.

## The Skill AI Can't Replace

Counterintuitively, "[AI makes systems thinking more valuable, not less](/)". AI generates code and suggests architectures, but cannot hold system intent or sense missing elements. That irreducibly human intuition (envisioning system shape before execution) separates component lists from coherent design.

This matters in domains where failures mean "[failed launch, a grounded fleet, a safety incident](/)", not 404 errors.
